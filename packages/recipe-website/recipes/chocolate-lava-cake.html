Molten Chocolate Lava Cake

Decadent individual chocolate cakes with a molten, gooey center. A restaurant-worthy dessert you can make at home in minutes.

20 min 2 servings Medium

Ingredients

  • 4 oz dark chocolate 70%, chopped
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 egg + 1 egg yolk
  • 3 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • Butter and cocoa powder for ramekins

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Butter two 6-oz ramekins and dust with cocoa powder, tapping out excess.

  2. Melt chocolate and butter together in a double boiler or microwave in 20-second bursts, stirring until smooth.

  3. Whisk egg, yolk, and sugar in a bowl until pale and thick, about 2 minutes.

  4. Fold chocolate mixture into eggs, then fold in flour and salt just until combined.

  5. Divide batter between ramekins. Bake 12–13 minutes — the edges should be firm but center still jiggly. Let rest 1 minute, then invert onto plates. Serve immediately.

Nutrition (per serving)

Calories420 kcal
Protein7g
Carbs38g
Fat28g

Chef's Tip

The timing is everything. 12 minutes gives you a true molten center; 14 minutes and the center sets. Use a kitchen timer and do not open the oven door during baking.
