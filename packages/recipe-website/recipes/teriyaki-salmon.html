Teriyaki Glazed Salmon

Glossy, savory-sweet teriyaki sauce over caramelized salmon fillets. Quick enough for weeknights, impressive enough for guests.

30 min 4 servings Easy

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets, 6 oz each
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • Sesame seeds and sliced green onion for garnish

Instructions

  1. Pat salmon dry and season lightly with salt.

  2. In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, and sesame oil. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.

  3. Whisk in cornstarch slurry and cook 1–2 minutes until glossy and thick.

  4. Set aside half the sauce for serving.

  5. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-high with a thin layer of oil. Sear salmon 3–4 minutes per side until caramelized and cooked through.

  6. Brush with reserved teriyaki sauce during the last minute of cooking.

  7. Serve with remaining sauce drizzled on top, garnished with sesame seeds and green onions.

Nutrition (per serving)

Calories380 kcal
Protein34g
Carbs18g
Fat20g

Chef's Tip

Don't move the salmon for the first 3 minutes of searing — let it develop a beautiful crust. The fish will release naturally from the pan when ready to flip.
