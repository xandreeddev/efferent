Lemon Herb Roasted Chicken

Golden, crispy skin with bright lemon and fragrant rosemary. A weeknight classic that feels special every time.

35 min 4 servings Medium

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves stripped
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 lb baby potatoes, halved

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Pat the chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels — this is the secret to crispy skin.

  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, minced garlic, chopped rosemary, smoked paprika, and a generous pinch of salt and pepper.

  3. Place the chicken thighs and halved potatoes on a rimmed baking sheet. Pour the marinade over everything and toss to coat evenly, making sure the skin side of the chicken faces up.

  4. Roast for 30–35 minutes, until the chicken skin is deeply golden and the internal temperature reaches 165°F. The potatoes should be tender and crisp-edged.

  5. Let rest for 5 minutes, then serve directly from the pan with an extra squeeze of lemon and a sprinkle of fresh rosemary.

Nutrition (per serving)

Calories420 kcal
Protein32g
Carbs24g
Fat22g

Chef's Tip

For extra-crispy skin, place the chicken on a wire rack set over the baking sheet. The hot air circulates underneath, rendering the fat beautifully. Save the pan drippings — they make an incredible sauce drizzled over the finished dish.
