Classic Pasta Pomodoro

A celebration of summer tomatoes, garlic, and basil tossed with perfectly al dente pasta. Simple, timeless, and deeply satisfying.

25 min 4 servings Easy

Ingredients

  • 1 lb spaghetti
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 can San Marzano tomatoes, crushed by hand
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves, torn
  • 1/2 cup Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated
  • Salt
  • Pinch of sugar

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook pasta 1 minute less than package directions for al dente.

  2. Meanwhile, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and cook until just golden, about 2 minutes — do not burn.

  3. Add crushed tomatoes, salt, and a pinch of sugar. Simmer 10 minutes until slightly thickened.

  4. Reserve 1 cup pasta water, then drain pasta. Add pasta to sauce with half the pasta water, tossing over medium heat for 1 minute.

  5. Remove from heat, stir in basil and Parmesan. Add more pasta water if needed for silkiness.

Nutrition (per serving)

Calories480 kcal
Protein16g
Carbs72g
Fat14g

Chef's Tip

San Marzano tomatoes make all the difference — their sweetness and low acidity create a sauce that needs almost no doctoring. Always finish pasta in the sauce, never just top it.
